Incucyte SX5-Sartorius

Detailed Technical specifications and related applications

Executive Summary

The Incucyte SX5-Sartorius is an advanced automated live-cell analysis system designed for real-time, kinetic monitoring of cell cultures directly within an incubator environment. This instrument provides continuous, non-invasive quantification of cellular proliferation, morphology, and protein expression via bright-field and multi-channel fluorescence imaging, enabling comprehensive longitudinal studies.

Core Operating Principle

The Incucyte SX5 operates on the principle of automated, time-lapse microscopy integrated within a standard cell culture incubator, maintaining physiological conditions while continuously imaging cells.

It utilizes a precision robotic stage to sequentially scan multiple wells or culture vessels, capturing high-resolution bright-field and fluorescence images at user-defined intervals. Proprietary image analysis software then processes these vast datasets to quantify dynamic cellular processes, such as confluence, cell migration, apoptosis, and reporter gene expression, over hours to weeks without manual intervention.

Component Breakdown

  • Optical Module: Interchangeable objectives (4x, 10x, 20x), sCMOS camera sensor, and multi-channel LED illumination (Green, Red, Far-Red).
  • Automated XY Stage: Precision stepper-motor stage for microplates (6-384 well) and flasks, ensuring reproducible imaging positions.
  • Integrated Processing: Embedded PC with RAID storage for high-throughput acquisition and real-time data management.
  • Software Suite: Algorithms for object segmentation, cell counting, and kinetic data visualization.

Step-by-Step Workflow

  1. Experimental Setup

    Cultures are prepared in compatible vessels and placed into the Incucyte within a CO2 incubator.

  2. Protocol Definition

    Define imaging frequency, duration, magnification, and specific fluorescence channels via software.

  3. Automated Imaging Cycle

    Robotic stage positions vessel; sCMOS camera captures high-res images for all defined channels.

  4. Data Acquisition & Analysis

    Raw data is timestamped and analyzed in real-time to quantify metrics like confluence and intensity.

  5. Visualization & Export

    Users access kinetic graphs and original images through the interface for real-time monitoring.

Common Failure Points

Optical Focus

Dust or alignment drift leading to blurry/inconsistent image data.

Mechanical Stage

Wear on motors or rails causing inaccurate plate positioning.

Illumination Degradation

LED aging or filter bleaching reducing signal-to-noise ratio.

Software Glitches

Firmware bugs or OS errors disrupting protocols and analysis.

Storage/Network

Disk failure or network overload leading to critical data loss.
